## Origins

Giant's Bridge is the result of my participation in the 2021 edition of [Summer Game Dev](https://summergamedev.sk/), a yearly game-making challenge taking place in Bratislava. I was brought onto the team as a game designer, and we were given 4 weeks to create a game that fit the theme 'Unexpected Side-Effect' and had to include power-ups. Our entry was Giant's Bridge. 

## My Role

At the beginning of the challenge, I was relegated largely to the role of a traditional game designer and team leader - I wrote a design document, created pitch sheets and slide decks, organised tasks and made sure the various visions within the team were all accounted for. I talked with our programmers and artists and made key gameplay and art decisions, and used those along with the jam requirements to settle on a final project idea that everyone was happy with. 

However, as the production went on, I ended up doing significantly more varied work. By the end of the project, roles were quite clear and team members were largely focused on completing their own tasks. As such, my role became much more varied. I spent a lot of time playtesting, and helping out programmers with placeholder art to prototype mechanics. I created music, worked on shaders, and took on a large part of the level design. Once or twice, I was even on mosquito-killing duty (this was the summer after all). 

## Results

The results for Summer Game Dev are split into two categories, each decided by a vote: A judges vote and a public vote. Much to our dismay, we lost the judges vote by 1 vote, and the public vote by a small handful. Because the results had been so close, we received an honorable mention at the awards ceremony, something that had not been done before.
