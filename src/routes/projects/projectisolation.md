## Origins

After abandoning 2 previous historically-focused shooters, I decided to abandon my obsession with historical accuracy and build a game just for it to be fun and relatively straightforward to build. My idea was Project Isolation. A yet-unnamed single player FPS Survival game, focused on exploration and survival in a relatively open world, giving a lot of agency to the player. 

This was both a way to work on systemic gameplay (which scales significantly better) and create an FPS that feels fresh, and isn't filled with waypoints, markers, and quests, but rather builds an intriguing world that the player wants to explore.

I also wanted to not rely on external assets too much, opting rather to figure out a way for me to produce all the assets in the game, be that through more efficient workflows or the use of procedural assets. I wanted to avoid a situation where I was relying too heavily on marketplace assets which did not fit together, causing the game to look very unprofessional.

## The Work So Far

As it stands, Project Isolation has changed wildly from it's initial concepts into something that's much more achievable. It's also been spun off into [FPS Core](/projects/fpscore) in order to provide a more user-friendly means of accessing the code that drives the game.

An initially open-world game has now switched to be 'semi-open world', where smaller explorable environments are connected together, each housing a unique point of interest required to progress to the next one. I've also had a lot less time to work on the project than I'd have hoped, due to the unforseen popularity of FPS Core. As such, the project is behind schedule. However, it's nonetheless been a very important learning resource, and has taught me a huge variety of things, such as:

- Modular game architecture
- Hard surface modelling
- Viewmodel Animation
- Sound Design
- Data-driven programming
- Git version control

Although the project is moving slowly at the moment, i'm still excited to keep working on it and bring it to market when it's ready.
